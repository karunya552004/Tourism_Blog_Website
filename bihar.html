1.Bodhgaya

Previous Next
Previous Next

Bodh Gaya is a village in the northeast Indian state of Bihar. Considered one of the most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites, it's dominated by the ancient brick Mahabodhi Temple Complex, built to mark the site where the Buddha attained enlightenment beneath a sacred Bodhi Tree. A direct descendant of the tree sits within the complex today, along with six other sacred sites, including a lotus pond.The Mahabodhi Temple Complex is one of the four holy sites related to the life of the Lord Buddha, and particularly to the attainment of Enlightenment. The first temple was built by Emperor Asoka in the 3rd century B.C., and the present temple dates from the 5th or 6th centuries.

2. Golghar

Previous Next

Golghar is the finest example of local craftsmanship. It was built under the command of Captain John Gartin in the year 1786 to be used as a granary. Golghar is nestled amidst the pristine river on one side, lush greens on one, and the entire city ahead of it. Entry to this stupendously constructed granary might be restricted, however, the public is still permitted to make use of the spiral staircase that leads one up the dome-shaped building. The panoramic views of the surroundings have led to visiting Golghar among the best things to do in Patna.

3. Gurudwara Patna Sahib

Previous Next

The bright white structure of this Gurudwara is certainly a treat. Also named as Takht Sri Patna Sahib, this place of worship was constructed under the commands of Maharaja Ranjit Singh. Gurudwara Patna Sahib exudes peace from every nook and visiting this religious spot is, hence, amongst the most heartwarming things to do in Patna, Bihar. This Gurudwara is devoted to the 10th Guru of Sikhism namely Guru Gobind Singh Ji. It is home to around five takhts or the sacred thrones which are considered holy in the religion.

4. Pataliputra

Previous Next

Being an ancient city, one can imagine the number of relics and timeless ruins that cover the city. One such iconic landmark from the golden days is Pataliputra. Sitting in Kumhrar, around 5km on the outskirts of the city, this monumental structure is the example of architecture from the Mauryan Empire. Falling among the best things to do in Patna, India for history aficionados, exploring the 80 pillars dating back to the years 1912 to 1915 is truly remarkable. Pataliputra Is a complete contrast from the modern-day city of Patna.

5.Ganga River

Previous Next

Ganga River is perhaps the holiest and most serene water body in Patna. The gentle flows of water, panoramic views of lush greens on the shores on the side, and the cityscapes on the other make an experience one of its kind. Among the top fun things to do in Patna with friends, families, or partners is taking a ride on the boat along the pristine waters of the Ganga. Patna is a city that is filled to the brim with crowds. This river gives an opportunity to escape the busy streets and spend some time in solitude. Make sure you visit during the late evenings, as dusk is on its verge.

6.Buddha Smriti Park

Previous Next

Buddha Smriti Park acts as a magnet to attract tourists. With an essence of spirituality spread all around, this park blankets a charcoal stupa namely Patliputra Karuna Stupa. For those who seek solace and serenity, this is the place to be at. Inaugurated by the 14th Dalai Lama, Buddha Smriti Park has plenty to offer. Apart from the charcoal stupa, one can also find saplings from the Bodhi Tree in Sri Lanka, Anuradhapura, and Bodhgaya. The Buddhist Museum, relaxing meditation hall, and a well-stocked library further makes this experience in Patna complete.

8.Agam Kuan

Previous Next

Agam Kuan is shrouded in mystery, owing to the fact that it is known as one of the oldest archaeological sites in Patna. It is believed that the Kuan was built during the Mauryan period and was built by Ashoka. The kuan is 105ft deep and has a perimeter of 20 ft and has eight arched windows. It is believed that the well was used as toruture chamber. There are many stories that are associated with the well, but today this well is considered very auspicious among Hindus.

9. Nalanda University

Previous Next

Nalanda University is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a trip to Patna is incomplete if you dont visit this site. The Nalanda University an ancient university and also a buddhist learning centre is considered as the longest running university in Indias history. The university dates back to the 5th Century CE (the Gupta period). Although in ruins, the red brick walls and the stupas tell the tales of the glorious history of Nalanda University, Patna and Bihar. Visiting this glorious ruins is one of the top things to do in Patna.
